<section class="about-observatory" aria-labelledby="about-observatory-title">
  <h2 id="about-observatory-title">About Me</h2>
  <p>I am a Ph.D. specializing in Physical Oceanography, Operational Oceanography, and ocean climate change impact, with a particular focus on <strong>ocean observations and data quality improvements, water mass and ocean circulation, ocean compound climate change, ocean warming monitoring, and compound risk assessment</strong>. I hold my Ph.D from the Institute of Atmospheric Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences (Supervisors: Prof. <a href="https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=XzerSxgAAAAJ&hl=en&oi=ao">CHENG Lijing</a> and <a href="http://www.ocean.iap.ac.cn">Prof. ZHU Jiang</a>) in July 2024.</p>
  <p>Currently, I am a post-doc investigator at Department of Geosciences (Laboratoire de Météorologie Dynamique), École Normale Supérieure (ENS), Université Paris Sciences et Lettres (PSL) since Feb 2025 (Advisors: Prof. <a href="https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=G0VWQsEAAAAJ&hl=en&oi=ao">Sabrina Speich</a> and Prof. <a href="https://scholar.google.com/citations?hl=en&user=MOA44_QAAAAJ&view_op=list_works&citft=1&email_for_op=tanzhetao19%40gmail.com&gmla=ANZ5fUMCfnT0QzegPfpwdSM8RTwmvgcly_vn8BGejH4rmU1Of-MumLFms-ETRhY1xK-ipXE4bAujtWHrnT0gHGX6x-TkNZ7TUvFCiLFUEWAQOK4MSt1sXRkUJ6zLnyMonkEvMv5ipIXvz7IwgQpC9FC-LUb8V0sQIJKnSLeJp8f4zcAxA7nGa3lv36445VhggyYrwLcJ3oDXHybZk9I6EU8X2xpDSXZFkpWaWQuR6K_9nQ1p">Elaine McDonagh</a>), working for Ice sheet impacts on global ocean circulation (WP5) of the OCEAN:ICE project funded by Horizon Europe.</p>
  <p>My research themes encompass operational oceanography, physical oceanography, and climate adaptation. Particularly, <strong>I mainly focused on the study of climate impact-drivers</strong> which connect physical ocean changes to broader climate impacts and marine governance (MPAs, BBNJ etc.). I constructed and utilized several ocean <em>in-situ</em> observational data products to identify climate hot spot regions and developed frameworks and tools to assess these impacts in different ocean sectors and ocean domains.</p>
  <p>Besides, <strong>I am the member of International Quality Controlled Ocean Database (IQuOD).</strong> I specialize in ocean data processing and analysis, significantly improving the quality of ocean observational data through open-source tools, data validation, quality control, XBT bias correction schemes, duplicate checking, and statistical analysis of climate data trends and variations.</p>
</section>

  <h2 id="updates-list-title">News in Tan et al., 2025, <em>Scientific Data</em></h2>

    <p class="updates-list__date">2025 · Dataset release</p>
    <h3>The global in-situ salinity profiles dataset since the 1940s was published in <em>Scientific Data</em> and is now freely available.</h3>
    <p><strong>Dataset:</strong> CODC-S can be downloaded via <a href="http://www.ocean.iap.ac.cn/ftp/cheng/CODCv2_Insitu_T_S_database/">IAP Ocean</a>.</p>
    <p>The second version (v2.1) of the CODC Global Ocean Temperature and Salinity Profile Science Database was led by the Institute of Atmospheric Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences, in collaboration with the Institute of Oceanology, Chinese Academy of Sciences, the Computer Network Information Center, Chinese Academy of Sciences, and other organizations. It contains 18,472,149 temperature profiles and <strong>11,273,326 salinity profiles</strong> from January 1940 to November 2024.</p>
    <p><strong>Citation:</strong> <u>Tan Z</u>, Zhu Y, Cheng L, Gouretski V, Pan Y, Yuan H, Wang Z, Li G, Song X, Zhang B, Bao S, Li Y, Zhu J. 2025. CODC-S: A quality-controlled global ocean salinity profiles dataset. <em>Scientific Data</em>, 12: 917. <a href="https://doi.org/10.1038/s41597-025-05172-9">https://doi.org/10.1038/s41597-025-05172-9</a></p>
